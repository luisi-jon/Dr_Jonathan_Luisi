General Description

Hexylbenzene is an aromatic hydrocarbon consisting of a benzene ring with a six-carbon straight-chain alkyl group (hexyl) attached. It is a colorless to light yellow liquid at room temperature with a faint aromatic odor. It is primarily used as an intermediate in organic synthesis, a solvent, and in the production of surfactants and lubricants.

Handling & Storage

Wear personal protective equipment. Ensure adequate ventilation. Avoid contact with skin, eyes, and clothing. Avoid ingestion and inhalation. Keep away from open flames, hot surfaces, and sources of ignition. Wash hands thoroughly after handling.

Storage Instructions

Keep containers tightly closed in a dry, cool, and well-ventilated place. Keep away from heat, sparks, and flame. Store away from strong oxidizing agents.

Research & Medical Insights

Therapeutic Aspects

"4-Hexyl-1,3-phenylenediol, a nuclear factor-κB inhibitor, improves photodamaged skin and clinical signs of ageing in a double-blinded, randomized controlled trial."

Clinical research identifies the hexylbenzene derivative 4-hexyl-1,3-phenylenediol (hexylresorcinol) as a potent NF-κB inhibitor. It is utilized therapeutically in topical treatments to reverse photodamage and signs of skin aging. Hexylbenzene also serves as a critical pharmaceutical building block in the synthesis of anticancer and neurological treatments.

The n-hexyl side chain of hexylbenzene is a structural feature of cannabidihexol (CBDH), a newly identified phytocannabinoid. Research indicates that this n-hexyl homolog possesses significant antinociceptive (pain-relieving) properties in vivo, contributing to the development of multi-target therapeutic strategies.

Hexylbenzene is classified as a neurotoxin. Occupational exposure can lead to acute solvent syndrome, characterized by central nervous system depression, dizziness, and narcosis. It is noted for its ability to cause systemic injury if entered into the bloodstream through skin lesions.

History, News & Regulatory Milestones

Historical Summary

Hexylbenzene (n-hexylbenzene) is a clear, colorless liquid alkylbenzene primarily used as a solvent, research chemical, and industrial intermediate. Its historical significance is rooted in the Friedel-Crafts alkylation reaction of 1877, which enabled the synthesis of long-chain alkylbenzenes. In the mid-20th century, it was identified as a naturally occurring flavor component in various cooked meats. In recent years, hexylbenzene has become a focus in atmospheric chemistry as a volatile organic compound (VOC) for urban health risk assessments and as a marker for food quality and beverage aroma profiling.
